多维度数据可视化项目

Multi-Dimensional Data Visualization

背景

Background

Our vision is limited to our physical senses. We were born in a three-dimensional environment. Without education of solid geometry or techniques of three-views, it is very hard for us to picture the interior structure of an object.

For most of us, the world is either two-dimensional or three-dimensional. Only some brilliant mind like Einstein himself or ancient Greek could be capable of picturing a world with four dimensions. The terminology of four dimensions sounds very self-explanatory; however, the visualization of a four-dimensional world is not as easy as how it sounds.

With an understanding of multi-dimensional structure, our way of thinking would progress to the next level. We will be able to understand incidents with a time frame as reference so that we know what had happened before, and why it happened this time. Otherwise, we can be very emotional about comments and judgements of the current incident. More importantly, we can also think about things on behalf of other entities, which would give us a comprehensive understanding of everything from mutiple angles.

数据库

Database

Two-dimensional database is very common that we almost practise it for our daily usage, for example, excel. Every entry can be easily pictured with one or two labels depending on the needs. The advantage of a multi-dimensional data over a two-dimensional one is well-explained in a guiding book of access, “Access 2016 Bible”.

To put it simple, you can bascially add more tags to an entry of data in a multi-dimensional database, whereas in two-dimensional scenario you will have to use cascading style to expand one tag in a separate sheet. If you understand both excel and access, you might know these already. However, if you don’t have prior knowledge, it can be bit of struggling for you to understand. This can be easily solved by using data visualization as a tool to further the step of understanding.

多维数据的应用

Why we need multi-dimensional database

Before we move on to the visulization of a multi-dimensional databas, we have to have a rough understanding of why we even need it. It comes to the concept of relational database, meaning every single entry of the data can be coorelated. For example, in educational field, a teaching manager needs to know which curriculum is used for which grade, which teacher is responsible of using which curriculum, or even maybe how many hours each teacher is assigned to. With all the information connected, we could easily facilitate the teacher with all the information he/she needs, for example, curriculum, class info, human resource info, payroll and so on.
