Thoughts of time

Maester
2016-09-18
time, 随笔
Writing

Prior knowledge

Time is not a constant(from the point of view of both physics and sociology). With prior knowledge of the variable property of time, does it mean that we are capable of defying time? If not, then what do we expect for, knowing the nature of time.

Physics

It’s not unobvious to specify the functionality of time, frame of reference for collective various purposes. Without a common reference, when how could people possibly meet each other without letting others waiting for too long? In general, it makes perfect sense for primative people to set up time reference for the benefits of time saving for both parties anybody involved.

Examples are never short for such proof, and sundial, a device applied by the relative position of the sun, is one of them. Errors can be ignored due to the relative distance between the sun and the earth, which makes shadows on sundials almost parallel to each other if placed at positions of similiar latitude. However, such time device will not be useful for locations of different latitudes.

Another example would be hourglass, but the restriction application of such device is more severe restricted than sundial. Hourglass is only useful in space visually or verbally communicable for both parties people involved. In the sense, the frame of reference of hourglass is simply not universal enough for a daily time span, but only useful for a short span period within limited space.

In both cases, identification of identifying a constant location becomes critical, the sun in the sundial case, and mutual presence of both parties in the hourglass case. Examples are numerous beyond count, and the point is the significance of constant reference is stressed.

The further question is whether that if the key of synchronization is time itself or location.

Philology

Literally, when we speak of time, we would always say “It takes four hours to ….”, omitting the center word “time”. Technically speaking, it should be “It takes four hours of time to ….”, under social language convention or collective recognition, we shorten it but are aware that we are talking about time.

However, if we are asked to explain the meaning of four hours, mostly, we would describe by using empirical experiences. Four hours is means two lectures, or two movies, or the whole afternoon’s work. In this case, the frame of reference is based on individual experience. A primative man would never have known about either movie or lecture, and in his case, he might say a hunting trip instead.

So the real question is are we actually talking about time as itself, or the mixture between our own experience and feelings?

Past tense

The very second when we speak, it has already happened. Thoughts develop somehow in our neural system and conveyed through via language. Despite the short interval of transmission between thinking and speaking, it has passed or already happened.

So what is time?
