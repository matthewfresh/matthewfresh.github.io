Thoughts of time

Maester
2016-09-18
time, 随笔
Writing

Prior knowledge

Time is not a constant.(from both physics and sociology point of view). With prior knowledge of the variable property of time, does it mean that we are capable of defying time? If not, then what do we expect for, knowing the nature of time.

Physics

It’s not unobvious to specify the functionality of time, frame of reference for collective purposes. Without a common reference, when could people possible meet each other without letting others waiting for too long. Generally, it makes sense for primative people to set up time reference for the benefits of time saving for both parties.

Examples are never short for such proof, and sundial is one of them, a device applied by the relative position of the sun. Errors can be ignored due to the relative distance between the sun and the earth, which makes shadows on sundials almost parallel to each other if placed at positions of similiar latitude. However, such time device will not be useful for locations of different latitudes.

Another example would be hourglass, but the restriction of such device is more severe than sundial. Hourglass is only useful in space visually or verbally communicable for both parties. In the sense, the frame of reference of hourglass is not universal enough for a daily time span, but only useful for a short span within limited space.

In both cases, identification of a constant location becomes critical, the sun in the sundial case, and mutual presence of both parties in the hourglass case. Examples are numerous beyond count, and the point is the significance of constant is stressed.

The further question is whether the key of synchronization is time itself or location.

Philology

Literally, when we speak of time, we would say “It takes four hours to ….”, omitting the center word “time”. Technically speaking, it should be “It takes four hours of time to ….”, under social convention or collective recognition, we shorten it but are aware that we are talking about time.

However, if we are asked to explain the meaning of four hours, mostly, we would describe by using empirical experience. Four hours is two lectures, two movies, or the whole afternoon work. In this case, the frame of reference is individual.

Are we talking about time as itself, or the mixture between our own experience and feelings?

Past tense

The very second when we speak, it has already happened. Thoughts develop somehow in our neurol system and conveyed through language. Despite the short interval of transmission between thinking and speaking, it has passed or happened.

So what is time?

Ancient Greek divides time into two, Chronos and Karios. The former denotes the quantitative nature of time and the latter referes to qualitative. One is chronological, sequential, and the other one is momentary, significant.
